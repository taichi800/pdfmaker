1. Character-by-Character Breakdown

2. Phrase-Level Analysis

是以 (shì yǐ) → "Indeed, that is why"

  • Common in **Legalist** and **Daoist** texts.
  • Appears in **Xunzi**, **Han Feizi**.

3. Sentence-Level Analysis

道可道，非常道。

Literal: "A method that can method is not a constant method."

Syntax:

道可道，非常道
nounmodalverbneg.adj.noun

4. Chapter-Level Themes

  • Paradox and self-negation as a rhetorical device.
  • Contrast between **constant (常)** and **temporary (可)**.

5. Relation to Other Chapters

Similar paradoxical structures appear in:

  • Chapter 2: "All things under heaven recognize beauty only because of ugliness."
  • Chapter 40: "Reversal is the movement of the Dao."

6. Meaning & Interpretation

Interpretations vary:

  • Daoist: The Dao is ineffable.
  • Confucian: Naming things imposes structure.

9. Historical Interpretations

Major interpretations:

  • Han Dynasty: Dao as a cosmic force.
  • Song Dynasty: Neo-Daoist metaphysics.

10. Practical Influence

Impact:
